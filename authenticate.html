Encryption alone protects you against someone passively reading your messages, but it doesn't protect you against someone actively impersonating the person you want to talk to. You need to authenticate the identity of your friend.

OTRon provides two ways to do this authentication: public key fingerprint verification, and keyphrase verification (also known as the socialist millionaire protocol). You only need to do one.

(Learn more about man-in-the-middle attacks.)

Fingerprint verification

Get your friend's public key fingerprint some other way, where you know it's them talking to you.

If you're talking to your friend now, share your fingerprint with them, so they can authenticate you on their side:

(Options page.)

Enter your friend's fingerprint here, and OTRon will match it against the fingerprint of the person you're talking to.

Secret phrase verification

You can also use the socialist millionaire protocol, which confirms the identities of you and your friend to each other by having you both enter in a shared secret passphrase. This passphrase should not be known to anyone who might be intercepting your conversation.
